1. The Appraisal Framework

The annual appraisal process categorizes employee performance into four distinct bands. These bands dictate the percentage of the annual hike, reflecting overall contribution, skill enhancement, and project delivery success during the financial year.

🏆

Band A

Exceptional Performer

Consistently exceeds all expectations. Takes proactive leadership, delivers critical value, and masters new competencies.

Estimated Hike 10% - 12%
🎯

Band B

Exceeds Expectations

Meets all and exceeds some goals. Highly dependable, consistent quality of work, and solid team contributor.

Estimated Hike 7% - 9%
⚖️

Band C

Meets Expectations

Delivers what is asked. Meets baseline project requirements but lacks proactive initiative beyond defined scope.

Estimated Hike 4% - 6%

Needs Improvement

📊 Organizational Performance Distribution

Appraisals often follow a relative normalization curve (forced ranking) to distribute budget allocations effectively. This model illustrates the typical distribution of employees across the four performance bands within a given cycle. Securing Band A places you in the top decile of performers.

Methodology & Variables

Projections are based on standard industry approximations for mid-level technical roles (C1/C2 grade). Actual hike percentages are subject to absolute business unit performance, individual project billing status, geographic location, and organizational fiscal policies in effect during the appraisal cycle. Data shown in historical context is aggregated for illustrative trend analysis.
